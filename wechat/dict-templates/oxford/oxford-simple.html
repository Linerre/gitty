Oxford Advanced Learner's Dictionary
zodiac
noun
/ˈzəʊdiæk/
1 the zodiac [singular] the imaginary area in the sky in which the sun, moon and planets appear to lie, and which has been divided into twelve equal parts, each with a special name and symbol
•  the signs of the zodiac
topics Space c1
▼ Word Origin

late Middle English: from Old French zodiaque, via Latin from Greek zōidiakos, from zōidion ‘sculptured animal figure’, diminutive of zōion ‘animal’.
